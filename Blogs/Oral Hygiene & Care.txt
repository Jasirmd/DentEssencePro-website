6. Can brushing too hard damage my teeth?
Gentle Brushing Tips by DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic, Bangalore
Yes, brushing too hard can harm your teeth and gums. While it may feel like you're cleaning better, aggressive brushing can actually wear down your enamel, irritate your gums, and cause long-term sensitivity.

At DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic in Tatanagar, Bangalore, we teach our patients how to brush gently and effectively for lifelong oral health.

❌ What Can Happen If You Brush Too Hard?
Gum Recession
– Gums pull away from the teeth, exposing sensitive root surfaces
Tooth Sensitivity
– Enamel loss leads to pain when eating hot, cold, or sweet foods
Tooth Abrasion
– Visible notches or grooves at the gum line (abrasive damage)
Erosion of Fillings and Dental Work
– Overbrushing can damage crowns, veneers, and bonding
✅ How to Brush Safely:
Use a soft-bristled toothbrush
Hold your brush like a pen—not a scrubber
Apply gentle pressure with small, circular motions
Brush for 2 minutes, twice a day
How to explain to a patient:
“Brushing harder doesn’t mean brushing better. It’s like washing a delicate cloth—you want to clean, not tear it. Let the brush and toothpaste do the work.”

Smart Brushing Habits from DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic, Bangalore

✅ Night (before bed — most important)

9. What is flossing?

Oral Hygiene Explained by DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic, Bangalore
Flossing is a dental hygiene practice that involves using a thin thread (dental floss) to remove plaque, food particles, and bacteria from between your teeth and under the gumline—areas your toothbrush cannot reach.

At DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic in Tatanagar, Bangalore, we recommend daily flossing to prevent gum disease, cavities, and bad breath.

✅ Why Is Flossing Important?
Even with perfect brushing, about 30–40% of tooth surfaces remain untouched. Flossing:

Prevents interdental decay
Protects against gum infections and bleeding
Reduces the risk of bad breath
Keeps crowns, bridges, and veneers clean
Types of Floss:
Waxed/Unwaxed String Floss – classic, effective
Floss Picks – easier for kids or beginners
Water Flossers – great for braces, implants, or sensitive gums
How to Floss Properly:
Take 12–18 inches of floss
Gently slide it between two teeth
Curve into a C-shape around each tooth
Slide up and down below the gumline
Use a fresh section for each space
How to explain to a patient:
“Flossing is like cleaning between your fingers after washing your hands—if you skip it, you’re leaving behind dirt you can’t see.”

12. How do I floss correctly?
Expert Hygiene Guide by DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic, Bangalore
Flossing is essential for maintaining healthy gums and preventing cavities between your teeth. At DentEssencePRO Dental Clinic in Tatanagar, Bangalore, we teach the correct flossing technique to make sure you're cleaning where your toothbrush can’t reach.

✅ Step-by-Step Guide: How to Floss Properly
Use Enough Floss
Break off about 12 to 18 inches of dental floss
Wind most of it around your middle fingers, leaving 1–2 inches between
Hold Floss Firmly
Grip the floss between your thumbs and index fingers
Keep it taut for control
Slide Gently Between Teeth
Guide the floss gently between two teeth
Avoid snapping or forcing it
Curve into a “C” Shape
Wrap the floss around one tooth
Slide it up and down against the tooth and under the gumline
Repeat on the Next Tooth
Curve the floss around the opposite tooth in the space
Use a clean section of floss for each tooth
Don’t Forget Back Teeth
Reach the very last molars carefully
? Pro Tips:
Floss once a day, preferably at night
Bleeding gums? Keep flossing gently—bleeding reduces as gums get healthier
Use floss picks or water flossers if string floss is difficult for you
?️ How to explain to a patient:
“Floss like you're hugging each tooth. It should slide, not snap. Wrap and sweep gently under the gumline for the best clean.”
